2043 is an era of complete quantification of lifestyle, with data monitoring and analysis becoming the dominant measure of well-being. Personal health optimisation is an encouraged social pursuit that directly correlates to ones social status and interaction with wider society. Vital Oasis is the most widely used health monitoring technology based on decades of development of healthcare digital devices since 2025. A discreet, bandage-like skin patch embedded with sensors that constantly monitor and analyse the users vitals, including heart rate, glucose level, nervous system and data correlation with daily actions. These results are linked to a software application where users daily schedule and actions are planned and managed in the pursuit of health optimisation. The patch prioritises user emotional stability, productivity and longevity through real-time adjustment of user diets, exercising levels, and social interactions. Restrictions on actions could be activated depending on the users ability to achieve particular standards of vital data, suggesting direct correlation between one's social status and their health statistics. In this future, self-care and optimisation are made possible with the development of fitness tracking systems and the quantification of health data. Bodily intuition and autonomy are rendered irrelevant, in which data algorithms hold authority over the standard of a healthy life.

History that paved the way

societal embrace of lifestyle quantification

Beginning in the 2020s, society has been embracing the use of algorithms and data as a source of grounded truth. The pursuit of perfection in health, productivity, and success became a reachable goal under the culture of self-surveillance through tracking quantified wellness acts, such as step counts, sleep patterns, and calorie calculations. The ideal lifestyle is tied to algorithmic feedback and statistics tracking by external devices and apps, leading to a growing dependency on data to make decisions for personal health.

Technology development
Health and fitness tracking devices have been increasing in popularity since the 2010s, with tools like Apple Watch and Fitbit introduced into the community with biometric tracking functions. They monitored user heart rate, sleeping patterns and step counts in the form of a detachable smart watch with sensors, conducting analysis and providing trend reports with health advices. These devices inspired the new forms of biometric technology, taking health assistance into a seamless, personalised experience in the form of sensory skin patches. The development of MC10 Biostamp has led to the successful creation of flexible sensory applications in the form of a thin, band-aid-like skin patch which blends into the skin and monitors a wide range of biometric data with cloud AI support. It supports continuous tracking of vital performance with real-time intervention that manages users health with data analysis and wellness modelling.

Mental Health Conflicts

Constant tracking and interventions build pressure for users to perform in a restricted and ideal manner, which could feed into individual obsession with numbers, fueling extreme self-consciousness and anxiety. User might connect self-worth to the reflection of data, particularly with their loss of intuition and the ability to think critically in terms of personal well-being due to over-reliance on external sources. With over-management of their lifestyle, it erases space for self-trust, rest and flexibility. It could lead to obsessive, abnormal behaviours in the pursuit of perfect health that go against natural bodily practices. While healthcare costs might decrease generally, mental health issues would increase exponentially over time.

Community and human connection

Emotional intimacy and connection could be reduced to statistics, people turning data points into social standards and dialogue cues, which could lead to the loss of empathy, understanding and nuanced feelings. It also builds competition between peers, comparison between productivity rates, performance scores, which could start off as motivation but turn into anxiety of being seen as ‘left behind’. Human relationships would become superficial and strained by subtle statistical rivalry, with solidarity being promoted to those who achieve and stay in its prioritised culture and pace.

Flattening Cultural Diversity

By promoting and intervening in users’ actions and habits, it would be based on a standardised wellness metric, which could be biased and exclusive to particular traditions. Cultural views on health and rituals could be dismissed as ‘inefficient’ or wrong, leading to the loss of cultural diversity in wellness.

Data and privacy leakage
The electronic device could also lead to high-profile cases of individuals hacking their electronic skin patch for altering their lifestyle plan and statistics under the surrounding pressure to achieve perfect numbers.
